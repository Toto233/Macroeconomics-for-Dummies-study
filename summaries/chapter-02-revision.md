# Chapter 2 Revision Block

## Three-Sentence Memory Version

Chapter 2 explains the big questions macroeconomists care about and the modelling approach they use to study them. It distinguishes core pairs of concepts such as real versus nominal, stock versus flow, income versus wealth, and level versus growth rate. It also introduces the interest rate as the price of money and shows why it matters for spending and investment.

- inflation versus price level
- real versus nominal
- stock versus flow
- income versus wealth
- why models are needed
- why intuition can fail
- why macro needs microfoundations
- why high interest rates tend to reduce consumption and investment

## Concept Comparisons

- `level` tells you how large a variable is at a point in time; `growth rate` tells you how fast it changes over time.
- `nominal` uses money amounts; `real` uses purchasing power or quantities.
- `income` is a flow; `wealth` is a stock.
- `debt level` is a stock; `new borrowing` is a flow.
- `price level` is a level; `inflation` is its growth rate.
