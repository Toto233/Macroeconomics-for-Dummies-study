## Concepts Covered This Session

- GDP is best understood first as total output over a period
- output, income, and expenditure are three aggregate views of the same economy-wide activity
- unsold production can still enter GDP through inventory accumulation
- GDP level and economic growth are different:
  - GDP is scale
  - growth usually refers to the growth rate of GDP
- nominal GDP can rise because of higher prices, not only because of higher output
- real GDP is a price-adjusted estimate meant to track output quantity more closely
- real GDP growth does not guarantee better household welfare or stronger economic sentiment
- sector weakness such as real-estate downturns can coexist with positive aggregate GDP growth
