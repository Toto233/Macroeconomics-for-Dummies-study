## Concepts Covered This Session

- GDP measures the market value of final goods and services produced within an economy over a period
- the same physical good can be an intermediate good or a final good depending on its use
- inventory produced this period counts in this period's GDP, while later sale of old inventory should not be counted again
- GDP records production activity, not whether the production was efficient, wise, or welfare-improving
- low-value or misallocated production can raise current GDP without creating lasting wealth
