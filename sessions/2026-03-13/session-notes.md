## Concepts Covered This Session

- a model is a simplified version of reality
- the point of a model is to isolate the mechanism relevant to the question
- whether a simplification is acceptable depends on the question being asked
- a model that is useful for explaining country averages may be poor for explaining differences across individuals
- average and median answer different questions rather than one always being superior
- inflation is the rate at which the overall price level rises
- high price level and high inflation are different ideas
